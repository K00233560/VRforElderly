Benefits of VR for Elderly

Promotes brain activity and positive mood

During the aging process brain activity tends to slow and memories can be hazy and difficult to access. The more of these memories that go, the harder it is to recall anything related due to the loss of trigger memories. In order to keep the brain active and responsive, it needs to be stimulated daily. Sometimes, a photograph or song can bring back some of those memories, but in the advanced aging process, even these triggers may not be sufficient. Virtual reality not only helps older adults to promote brain activity, but it has also been shown to boost their mood. Nursing homes residents that have tried virtual reality across the globe, such as those at ST RAPHAEL'S in East Sussex and Mercy Health, Melbourne, have reported that they feel less bored and depressed when interacting with the device. In addition, the results of a study called “Impact of Virtual Reality (VR) Experience on Older Adults’ Well-Being”, have deduced that “participants that used the VR system reported being less socially isolated, being less likely to show signs of depression, experiencing positive affects more frequently, and feeling better about their overall well-being”.

Promotes Movement

Because virtual reality is immersive and interactive, it helps to promote movement. VR headsets can be used with hand-held controls that allow the user to interact with the simulated environment, keeping them active regardless of being chairbound, bedbound, or having other impairments.

Promotes Socialisation

Social isolation and loneliness are found to be health risks among older adults. Socialisation is vital in older adults, creating a sense of purpose, feelings of belonging, increased self-esteem and improved physical and mental health. Sharing virtual reality experiences can help promote communication amongst older adults, especially when conversation is sometimes limited.
Group virtual reality is a good method for encouraging older adults to mingle. A number of older adults can wear different VR headsets and bond over a shared virtual experience at the same time. Using virtual reality not only helps promote unity but also decrease loneliness in older adults, improving their quality of life overall.
